Working with Women

HerFiber project is tailored to train and support women groups in banana and plantain growing communities, integrate them into clusters to extract fiber from banana and plantain stems to yarn, weaving into fabric for eco-friendly packaging bag production.

The goal of HerFiber is to stimulate the participation of women in the circular economy to improve their livelihoods and promote environmental sustainability.

Working with Girls

Eco-Girls support scheme is designed to train, equip and empower girls with practical bag design and making skills using recyclable and biodegradable materials. The goal is to transform young bag makers to change makers who are passionate about the elimination of single-use plastics through production of Eco-friendly shopping bags.

Narrative Building

Our Goal in the Naija Zero Plastic Challenge

To engage content creators to deepen the awareness of adopting a zero plastic lifestyle that encourages behavioral change and a leap towards smart eco-friendly alternatives.

The Challenge seeks to achieve the following objectives:

Build a narrative using creative and digital channels to drive the adoption of a zero plastic lifestyle in Nigeria. Create awareness about the negative impact of plastic waste on our environment and the need for the adoption of eco-friendly and smart alternatives to plastics. Deepen stakeholders participation in the advocacy for a circular economy.

Entry Categories

The Challenge is designed to deepen zero plastic narrative using creative media such as Visual Art, Spoken Word, Short Video, Music and Photography.

How we work with content creators

Storytellers have a role to play in the transition to a circular economy through leaning their voices to raise awareness about the negative impact of plastic wastes on our landfills and water bodies.

Doing away with plastic shopping bags requires behavioral change. That is where narrative building comes in.
